Questions brokers ask

Fair questions, straight answers

Who actually writes it?

Frontier AI does the drafting — that's why 72 hours and $500 are possible — governed by a written production methodology (section rubrics, financial-narrative rules, sourcing standards, a banned-claims list), with every deliverable reviewed by a human principal before it reaches you. Then you review it, which is the real gate: nothing goes to a buyer without a professional broker's sign-off — yours.

My client's financials are sensitive. Where do they go?

Into the drafting engagement and nowhere else: processed under commercial AI terms that don't train on your data, never placed in any public asset, deleted on request. We'll sign your NDA before you send a page, or use our mutual template. The fictional sample exists precisely so no client data ever needs to appear in marketing.

Will it read like AI wrote it?

Read the sample and decide — that's what it's for. The methodology bans the tells (hype adjectives, hedge-everything mush, boilerplate market sections) and enforces the things buyers actually check: numbers that foot, claims that trace, headwinds stated honestly with their mitigations.

Do you do valuations or broker opinions of value?

No, categorically. We present your recast and your asking price as supplied. That line protects you (it's your license and your pricing craft) and us (we're a drafting service, not an appraiser).

What do you need from me, exactly?

Three things: your recast/normalized P&L (3 years), your add-back schedule, and the completed seller questionnaire — ours or your own form. Photos and brand kit if you want them used. The 72-hour clock starts when intake is complete.

What if my listing is in an industry you haven't written?

The methodology is industry-agnostic; the research standard isn't thinner for unusual industries — it just cites different sources. If we genuinely can't hit the quality bar for a niche, we'll say so before you send anything, not after.
